# Stroke

Stroke is a serious medical emergency that occurs when blood flow to the brain is interrupted, depriving brain cells of oxygen and nutrients. It is one of the leading causes of disability and death worldwide. Common risk factors include hypertension, diabetes, high cholesterol, obesity, smoking, alcohol consumption, and advancing age.

# Types of Strokes

- Ischemic Stroke (85%) – Caused by blockage of blood vessels supplying the brain.
- Haemorrhagic Stroke (15%) – Caused by bleeding within or around the brain.

# Common Warning Signs

- Sudden weakness or numbness of the face, arm, or leg
- Difficulty speaking or understanding speech
- Sudden vision problems
- Loss of balance, dizziness, or severe headache

# Importance of Early Intervention

The FAST method helps identify stroke symptoms:

- F – Face drooping
- A – Arm weakness
- S – Speech difficulty
- T – Time to seek emergency medical help
