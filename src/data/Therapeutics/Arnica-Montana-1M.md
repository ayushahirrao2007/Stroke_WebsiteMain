# Overview

Stroke is one of the leading causes of disability worldwide, often leaving patients with long-term challenges such as weakness, impaired speech, numbness, dizziness, and reduced quality of life. While conventional treatment focuses on emergency care and rehabilitation, complementary therapies may help support the recovery process.

# About the Study

An observational clinical study evaluated the role of Arnica montana 1M as an adjunct to standard stroke management in 15 patients diagnosed with ischemic stroke, hemorrhagic stroke, or transient ischemic attack (TIA). Patients were monitored over a period of two months with regular follow-up assessments.

# Key Findings

- 86.67% of patients showed clinical improvement during the study period.
- Patients with hemorrhagic stroke demonstrated notable improvements in motor function and speech.
- Most patients with TIA reported reduced dizziness, weakness, and improved overall well-being.
- Patients with ischemic stroke showed limited neurological recovery within the short observation period.
- No significant adverse effects related to Arnica montana 1M were reported.

# Potential Benefits Observed

- Improved motor coordination and muscle strength
- Better speech clarity and communication
- Reduction in weakness, dizziness, and fatigue
- Enhanced functional ability in daily activities
- Emotional and psychological support during recovery

# Conclusion

The study suggests that Arnica montana 1M may serve as a supportive complementary therapy in post-stroke rehabilitation, particularly for patients recovering from hemorrhagic stroke and transient ischemic attacks. However, larger controlled clinical trials are needed to further establish its effectiveness and role alongside conventional stroke rehabilitation programs.

# Disclaimer

Stroke is a medical emergency that requires immediate conventional medical treatment. Homoeopathic medicines should be used only as a complementary approach under the supervision of qualified healthcare professionals and should not replace standard medical care.
